“ My castings are an exhibit of my observations. In my work I try to mimic the strength and stability of urban structures while also evoking the tranquility and power of nature. The materials I use abstract and echo this theme. Glass, copper, and concrete are often associated with ridged city buildings; while sand is the raw, untreated predecessor of glass.

The simplicity of line is how I create abstract compositions, the result is a contrast between positive and negative space. In the case of my castings, both materials are manipulated, but the sculptures themselves are raw, untreated, and organic. Light plays upon the different textures, creating a seemingly emotional landscape that both glows and reflects. ”
